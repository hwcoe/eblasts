University of Florida launches its first engineering extension office

As a land-grant university, it is our mission at the University of Florida to aid in our state’s economic development and to help our citizens elevate their well-being. For decades, UF's College of Agriculture has led that charge. The 21st century has changed the landscape of Florida, and the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering is responding by launching UF Innovation Station Sarasota County – the first extension office of our new Florida Engineering Experiment Station Network.

This public-private collaboration includes support from the county, local economic development agencies and private donors, as well as participation from schools, local colleges, startups and businesses in the region. Together, we will create a talent and technology pipeline that will develop our workforce and improve our tech transfer. We will strengthen Florida’s role in the global innovation economy.

If you look at the major innovation hubs in this country you will find that they occur where the largest number of engineering graduates are being produced. With a $300 million transformation underway, the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering is expanding faculty and infrastructure to accommodate an increase in student enrollment to 10,000 per year. Our goal is to keep that talent instate and keep our high-tech jobs from going offshore.

To find out more about our plans to strengthen Florida’s role in the global innovation economy, visit www.eng.ufl.edu/FLEXStation.
