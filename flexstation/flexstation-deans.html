University of Florida launches its first engineering extension office
The Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering proudly introduces the UF Innovation Station Sarasota County – our first extension office of the new Florida Engineering Experiment Station Network.
As Florida’s land-grant university, UF is dedicated to helping drive the state’s economic development. We are using our powerful engineering education and research engine to collaborate with:
• County and local governments
• Economic development agencies
• Private donors invested in the region
• K-12 schools and local colleges
• Area startups and established businesses
We have created a talent and technology pipeline with area industry that will develop our workforce and boost tech transfer. We will propel Florida forward in the global innovation economy.
“I’m proud that our College of Engineering has chosen this unique path of putting its research, talent and engineering expertise behind our state’s innovation communities – starting right here in Sarasota with our engineering extension office, UF Innovation Station Sarasota County.”
- UF President Kent Fuchs
“If you look at the major innovation hubs in this country you will find that they occur where the largest number of engineering graduates are being produced.” - Cammy Abernathy, dean
With a $300 million transformation underway, the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering is expanding faculty and infrastructure to accommodate 10,000 students per year. Our goal is to keep that talent in state and keep our high-tech jobs from going offshore.
To find out more about our plans to strengthen Florida’s role in the global innovation economy, visit www.eng.ufl.edu/FLEXStation.
