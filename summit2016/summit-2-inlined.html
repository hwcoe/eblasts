Registration is open – reserve your space now!
Join the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering and the preeminent women of the Committee of 200 (C200) at the C200 STEM Outreach Event and Leadership Summit for a discussion on the role of women in technology and STEM and the power to lead the next generation of innovative solutions for a better society.

Keynotes

Transforming the Future as Women

Linda Hudson, UF Distinguished Alumna & CEO, The Cardea Group

Women pioneers are prepared to be on the forefront of change. What are the best strategies and practices for the next generation of women leaders to effectively shape the future?

Women in Technology – Why Now?

Shellye Archambeau, CEO, MetricSteam

The technology industry is changing. Women are taking on greater leadership roles and are making up more of the STEM workforce. What does this mean for 2016 and beyond?

Why Women Engineers Make the Best CEOs

Tanya Fratto, Board Member, Boart Longyear, Smiths Group and American Drainage Systems (ADS)

How can engineers use their unique background blend of STEM education and interdisciplinary experiences to elevate their leadership roles?
Panels
The Value of Diversity and Inclusion in STEM
The inclusion of women and minorities adds to the richness of ideas and creativity, particularly in academic and STEM fields. Panelists will discuss how inclusive environments add value to the workplace.
What Happens When Life Happens
Panelists will share experiences on how they’ve defied the odds to become women leaders, and how they’ve reimagined their roles in both their careers and private lives.
Creating and Sustaining a Culture of Innovation
Panelists will discuss ways to create and sustain a work environment that organically fosters innovative thinking. How can leaders encourage their employees to be creative in their daily tasks to sustain long-term advancement?

C200 is a membership organization of the world’s most successful women entrepreneurs and corporate innovators. C200 has more than 400 members who collectively generate more than $1.4 trillion in annual revenues.
About the UF Engineering Summit

The UF Engineering Summit has been organized annually by the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering since 2010. Under alternating themes of leadership and innovation, the summit is where engineering alumni, faculty members and students, alongside world-renowned thought leaders, connect and engage to inspire new ways of approaching and solving problems.
