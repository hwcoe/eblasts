Sequestration Information

The Budget Control Act of 2011 (i.e. Sequestration), became effective March 1 and imposed automatic cuts of 5.1 percent in nondefense and 7.3 percent in defense discretionary spending in the FY13 federal budget. The UF Office of Research estimates that these cuts in federally funded research will result in a drop of approximately $14M in research funding to UF, which includes an overall drop of 4% in IDC. In anticipation of this drop in overall IDC, President Machen has asked the Office of Research to absorb a 4% budget cut in FY14 given that the budget for that office principally comes from IDC. As part of this cut, the Office of Research will be reducing support provided to the Major Analytical Instrumentation Center (MAIC) and to the Nanoscale Research Facility (NRF) in FY14. The amount to be provided to MAIC will be $173,000 as compared to $184,000 that was provided last year and to NRF will be $94,000 as compared to $100,000 that was provided last year. Decisions on how to absorb the expected decline in IDC allocated to the college will be made after the final E&G budget is given to the college in late spring.

Action Items From the Operations Advisory Committee (OAC)

The OAC has made a number of suggestions on how to improve research management in the college. The following suggestions have been addressed:

Request: Updates should be available for PI review in order to keep PIs informed of the status of pending negotiations

Action Taken: OER logs are available for PIs to view. If you would like more information on how to access this database please contact OER.

Request: Projects which have been under negotiation for more than two months should be flagged for special attention by the college

Action Taken: Jennifer Curtis and Roz Heath now review the status of pending negotiations weekly. Projects which have been under negotiation for more than two months are given special attention to facilitate completion.

The following items have been identified by the OAC for attention and are currently in the process of being addressed:

An online form for ITAR

A web page containing a summary of all PI training

A website listing historical company research

STUDENT NEWS & EVENTS

2013 UF Presidential Service Awards

Two engineering students were honored at the President's House for their past year's service work in the community:

Akua Oppong-Anane, a PhD candidate in the department of Environmental Engineering Sciences, volunteered over 60 hours of her time to help library patrons strengthen their computer skills and literacy. As a science fair judge at a local middle school, she also mentored students on their research processes and methodologies.

Chris Cruise, a second-year mechanical engineering student, spent 300 hours helping an Ocala high school team prepare for the regional FIRST (For Inspiration and Recognition of Science and Technology) Robotics Competition in Orlando.
