The UF Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering Launches First Innovation Station in Sarasota County

With a bold vision for accelerating growth in this region's innovation economy, the University of Florida Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering will open the first UF Innovation Station in Sarasota County. It will be the first physical extension of UF's Florida Engineering Experiment Station (FLEXStation), affording businesses and entrepreneurs unprecedented access to tech workforce talent, applied research, faculty and intellectual property. UFTI Welcomes New Transportation Technology Transfer (T2) Center Director

Maria A. Cahill, AICP will take the position as the T2 Center Director in April 2016. She will lead the Center, which houses the Local Technical Assistance Program (LTAP), the Center for Transportation Training (CTT), Florida’s Pedestrian and Bicycling Safety Resource Center, the Florida Occupant Protection Resource Center, and several other dynamic grant-driven as well as revenue-supported programs. Cahill earned her B.A. in Political Science from the University of Florida, as well as her M.A. in Urban and Regional Planning with a focus on community planning, growth management and concurrency. She started her career as a local planner with Monroe County and later managed the Florida Keys Area of Critical State Concern program prior to moving to Tallahassee. Read more»

STAFF SUCCESS

ESSIE was named an American Concrete Institute (ACI) Outstanding University for 2015. This award recognizes the school as being one of the top 16 programs in the nation with regards to ACI-related activities. This award is made possible by the diligent staff, faculty and students in ESSIE.

(From left) Amy Haberman, Yolanda Hankerson, Deb Mayhew and Paul Rocha were each selected for a 2016 Superior Accomplishment Award. This competitive and prestigious award is given to staff and faculty who go “the extra mile and beyond” in normal, assigned duties.

Two teams of engineering staff members (representing ESSIE and MSE) participated in the campus-wide 2016 Spring Walking Challenge. More than 25 engineering staff recorded their daily steps throughout the challenge and worked consciously toward living an active lifestyle. The MSE team ranked in the top 10 for achieving nearly 375,000 steps.
