AUGUST XX, 2017

COLLEGE NEWS

Toshi Nishida named new interim Associate Dean of Academic Affairs

Toshikazu “Toshi” Nishida, associate chair of research in ECE and affiliate professor in MAE, began his role as Interim Associate Dean for Academic Affairs on July 15, 2017. Nishida serves as director of the MIST Center and is a co-founder of the Interdisciplinary Microsystems Group. As an integral member of the college leadership team, Nishida will oversee academic affairs and graduate programs. Read more»

Wes Bolch, who previously served in this role, has returned to full-time faculty status and will continue his ongoing research activities in the areas of medical physics and radiological engineering. He is currently working with college leadership to develop a new program in Crisis Leadership Engineering where he will take the lead in the area of radiological response to nuclear and terrorist incidents.

Richard Dickinson Joins National Science Foundation

Richard Dickinson, chair of the Department of Chemical Engineering (ChemE), will be joining the National Science Foundation to serve as director of the Chemical, Biological, Environmental, and Transport Systems (CBET) Division, beginning August 16, 2017. With 16 programs and a budget of nearly $200 million, the CBET division is an essential source of NSF research funding. As a rotator, Dickinson will retain his UF faculty position and return to campus after his appointment in Washington.

Carlos Rinaldi to Serve as Interim Chair of CHE

Carlos Rinaldi has agreed to serve as interim chair of ChemE, effective August 1, 2017. Rinaldi is currently the Charles A. Stokes Term Professor in Chemical Engineering, with a joint appointment in BME. His research is focused on advancing the understanding and biomedical applications of suspensions of magnetic nanoparticles.

INSTITUTE NEWS

IoT expert joins the Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World

UF’s newest institute, which focuses on the Internet of Things, has hired Dr. Yier Jin as its first faculty member. Yier Jin spoke at a campus-wide seminar about the security and privacy challenges in IoT. The standing-room only event was attended by special guests including the naming benefactor David Nelms and his family. Read more»

Erik Sander named the Michael Durham Director of the Engineering Innovation Institute

Erik Sander, founding director of EII, is now the Michael Durham Director of the Engineering Innovation Institute thanks to the support of distinguished ESSIE alumnus, Michael Durham. The contributions of this named directorship will continue to expand the EII programs to empower sustainable solutions by UF engineers. Read more»

The Institute for Excellence and Engineering Education (IE3) represents UF at ASEE conference

Several college faculty, including IE3 director Hans van Oostrom, attended the the American Society for Engineering Education’s conference in Ohio to represent our college’s vision for delivering excellence in research and education. Read more»

FLEXStation showcases innovation and leadership at eMerge Americas

eMerge Americas is a technology conference held in Miami each year that engages thousands of people. This year, FLEXStation set up a booth and shared news about our engineering extension efforts. Professor Jennifer Rice, ESSIE, was a guest panelist at the event. She addressed the infrastructure challenges of vulnerable coastal communities. Read more»
