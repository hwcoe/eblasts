Kevin R. B. Butler, Ph.D., CISE, a holder of the Arnold and Lisa Goldberg Rising Star Associate Professor in Computer Science, has been selected as a 2020 Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering Doctoral Dissertation Advisor/Mentoring Awardee.

Dickinson Elected American Institute of Chemical Engineers Fellow

Richard B. Dickinson, Ph.D., CHE, was elected a Fellow of the American Institute of Chemical Engineers (AIChE). This honor is the organization’s highest grade of membership and is awarded to members in recognition of significant professional accomplishments and contributions in chemical engineering.

Faculty Recognized With NSF CAREER Awards

Sharon Lynn Chu, Ph.D., CISE, has received a CAREER award from the National Science Foundation (NSF). Her project will investigate the design of wearable technologies to connect learning across formal and informal contexts, especially for elementary and middle school students, focusing on science topics.

Matthew Hale, Ph.D., and Ryan Houim, Ph.D., both MAE, have received NSF Early CAREER Awards in 2020. Dr. Hale’s project, “A Unified Theory of Private Control Systems,” will research the development of new models, mathematical tools, and computational algorithms to address emerging complex multi-agent systems such as remote sensing, economic dispatch models with renewable energy, and efficiency estimation in transportation networks. Dr. Houim’s project, “Understanding the Influence of Thermal Radiation on the Ignition and Propagation of Dust Explosions,” will study the influence of thermal radiation on dust explosions using state-of-the-art granular multiphase models, radiation approximations, and numerical simulation techniques.

Xiaoyu Song, Ph.D., ESSIE, received an NSF Faculty Early CAREER award to study failures in unsaturated porous media. He will use the five-year, $500,000 award to formulate and validate a novel computational mathematical paradigm to evaluate failures in unsaturated soils impacted by climate change.

Tuba Yavuz, Ph.D., ECE, has been awarded a 2020 NSF CAREER Award for her project “Towards a Secure and Reliable Internet of Things through Automated Model Extraction and Analysis.” The project will also help broaden participation of women and other underrepresented groups in IoT security, formal methods, and software engineering research.

UF Professors Elected as AIMBE Fellows

Congratulations to Josephine Allen, Ph.D., MSE; Wesley Bolch Ph.D., BME; Kevin Otto, Ph.D., BME; Carlos Rinaldi, Ph.D., CHE; and Lakiesha Williams, Ph.D., BME, who were elected to the American Institute for Medical and Biological Engineering (AIMBE) College of Fellows on March 24, 2020.

Breanne Schenk, left, and Juan Griego, of UF’s Fab Lab, work on removing 3D-printed filter caps for respirator masks produced by Altavian. (Photo courtesy UF’s DCP Fabrication Lab)

UF-Led Ventilator Design Clears First FDA Review

A ventilator envisioned by UF engineer and Anesthesiology faculty, Samsun Lampotang, Ph.D., (MAE ’92) built with do-it-yourself parts has gained first-step FDA authorization, offering hope that it could be in use within weeks at hospitals around the world where COVID-19 patients have overwhelmed supplies of traditional medical equipment.
