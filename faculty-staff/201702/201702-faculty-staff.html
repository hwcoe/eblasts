COLLEGE NEWS

Michele Manuel named chair of MSE

Effective February 1, 2017, Michele Manuel will lead the Materials Science and Engineering Department as Chair. Manuel is a recognized leader and expert in light metals, design and computational methodologies for materials development, and materials characterization. She currently holds the Rolf E. Hummel Professorship of Materials Science and Engineering. Manuel received her Ph.D. in MSE from Northwestern University and her B.S. in MSE at UF. She is the recipient of the Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE), NSF CAREER Award, NASA Early Career Faculty Award and more.

Incoming leaders bring diverse experience to Dean’s Advisory Board

After a full four-year term, which included establishing the organizational structure to help steer the college vision and support Dean Cammy Abernathy’s leadership team, Carlos del Sol has stepped down as chair of the Dean’s Advisory Board (DAB).

International patent attorney Bill Troner has stepped into the chair position, adding to a growing list of ways he’s served the college. He previously served as the vice chair of the DAB, and was also the co-chair of the board’s Development committee.

Gary Miller is the board’s new vice chair, bringing academic and industry experience to the position. Gary is a double-Gator with a bachelor’s and Ph.D. degree from MAE. He is a co-founder and the current vice president of research and development of Exactech and serves on the external advisory boards for BME and MAE. Read more»
