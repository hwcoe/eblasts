COLLEGE NEWS

Carlos Rinaldi Named Chair of the Department of Chemical Engineering

Dr. Rinaldi is the Charles A. Stokes Term Professor at the J. Crayton Pruitt Family Department of Biomedical Engineering. He is an internationally renowned scholar for his significant contributions to the field of bionanomedicine through the advancement of biomedical applications of magnetic nanoparticles, specifically cancer treatment through magnetic fluid hyperthermia. His scholarly achievements have earned him numerous awards, including the Presidential Early Career Award for Scientists and Engineers (PECASE), the International Journal of Nanomedicine Early Career Award for outstanding contributions to the field of nanomedicine, the Merck Health Innovation Award in Engineering and the NSF CAREER Award. At UF, Rinaldi is also recognized for his dedication and service to faculty and students – he has been awarded a University of Florida Term Professorship, the Department of Chemical Engineering Excellence in Service Award, and the J. Crayton Pruitt Family Department of Biomedical Engineering Faculty Service Award. Carlos received his bachelor's degree in Chemical Engineering at the University of Puerto Rico, Mayagüez and completed degrees in Master of Science in Chemical Engineering, Master of Science in Chemical Engineering Practice and Doctor of Philosophy in Chemical Engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. He was the Interim Chair at CHE since August 2017 and the Senior Associate Chair at BME from January 2016 to July 2017. Prior to joining UF in 2012, Carlos was Assistant Station Director at the MIT David H. Koch School of Chemical Engineering Practice and Professor at the Department of Chemical Engineering at the University of Puerto Rico, Mayagüez.
