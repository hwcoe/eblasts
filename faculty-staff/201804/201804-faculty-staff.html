COLLEGE NEWS

Stakeholders Dinner 2018

Gator Engineering alumni and friends gathered at the Hilton UF Conference Center to celebrate 108 years of dedicated service, selfless mentorship and loyal support to the college.

Katherine Dodge, ESSIE, and Myra Kurosu Jalil, MAE, spoke about the impact of receiving, respectively, the Bruce and Winnie Rogow Undergraduate Scholar Inaugural Award and the Robert and Jill Peterson Women's Scholarship Inaugural Award.

Lifelong supporters Don Irons, Harlan Harrell and Frank Townsend were inducted into the Benton Loyalty Society for their unwavering support to the college for more than 25 years.

UF Honored with ASEE Engineering Excellence for Veterans Award

HWCOE has been recognized with a 2018 Engineering Excellence for Veterans Award from the American Society of Engineering Education (ASEE). This award is given to recognize academic institutions that are leaders in helping veterans bridge the gap between military and civilian worlds.

2018 Commencement Honorees

Dr. Herbert “Herbie” Wertheim, honorary chairman of the college, will be awarded the Honorary Doctorate in Technology Degree from UF at the doctoral ceremony on May 4, 9:00 a.m. at the Exactech Area in the Stephen O'Connell Center.

Alex Moreno, CHE, will be honored with the college’s Distinguished Alumnus Award at the engineering bachelor’s ceremony on May 6, 7:00 p.m. at the Exactech Area in the Stephen O'Connell Center.

UF to House the First NSF Center on Big Learning

After over two years of development across academic institutions and industry partners, the NSF Center for Big Learning (CBL) has been officially awarded to UF. As the first NSF center on big learning, the CBL will be an NSF Industry & University Cooperative Research Center (I/UCRC) and will be housed on the fourth floor of the New Engineering Building (NEB).

UF joins MetroLab Network with the City of Gainesville

The collaboration will focus on three research initiatives: 1) an autonomous shuttle between campus and downtown Gainesville; 2) finding better ways to connect patients with local healthcare systems; and 3) a smart street lighting project with sensor technology.

UF Participated in the USA Science & Engineering Festival in Washington DC

CISE students and alumni showcased UF innovations at the nation’s largest gathering of STEM exhibits, shows and hands-on activities.

One in Five Older Adults Experience Brain Network Weakening Following Knee Replacement Surgery

A UF study that includes Mingzhou Ding, BME, finds that 23 percent of adults age 60 and older who underwent a total knee replacement experienced a decline in activity in at least one region of the brain responsible for specific cognitive functions.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Kyle Allen, BME, received a $1.8M grant from the National Institutes of Health to identify and investigate sources of osteoarthritis and conditions of tissue deterioration. The research will focus on looking beyond the articular cartilage to see what changes in which specific tissues are really driving the symptomology of osteoarthritis.

Jaime Ruiz, CISE, Parisa Rashidi, BME and Roozbeh Tabrisian, ECE, received NSF CAREER Awards. They join more than 20 active CAREER projects at the college. NEED LINK

Daniel Ferris, BME, received a $2M NIH grant to investigate how the human brain controls locomotor adaptation under varying conditions. Information gained from this study could aid in developing new methods of diagnosis and treatment for gait disabilities.
