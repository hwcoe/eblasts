COLLEGE NEWS

New Opportunities for College Leadership; Search for Associate Deans Has Begun

Jennifer Curtis selected as Dean of Engineering for UC Davis

Our associate dean of research and facilities will become the new dean of engineering for the University of California at Davis later this fall. As associate dean, Jennifer Curtis, Ph.D., has supported faculty and researchers in developing and maintaining research programs, including promoting interdisciplinary research, working with research sponsors and funding agencies, coordinating the college’s research institutes and ensuring regulatory compliance. She has created a new COE Office of Laboratory Safety and implemented new organizational and fee structures for the college’s Research Service Centers. Jennifer has also served as director of the Florida Energy Systems Consortium, which promotes collaboration between Florida’s public universities to conduct research, education and commercialization in energy technology.

Angela Lindner selected as UF’s Associate Provost
for Undergraduate Affairs

Our associate dean of student affairs will become the new associate provost for undergraduate affairs and director of professional curriculum this month. Angela Lindner, Ph.D., has been responsible for oversight of the college admission and certification processes, developing and implementing effective academic advising and counseling for approximately 6,500 engineering students, administering undergraduate curricular and assessment programs, as well as K-12 outreach and undergraduate co-curricular programs. Under her guidance, the college shifted to a centralized first-year advising model that will be fully realized this year. Angela was instrumental in developing the Gator Engineering @ Santa Fe Program, launching of the Global Innovation Network with partner schools, creating the Art in Engineering Museum Night at the Harn, and beginning the tradition of the Gator Engineering Attributes Awards Program. Though she will be missed at the college, her experience in student retention, diversity, enrollment, and experiential learning will be most appreciated in UF’s Office of Undergraduate Affairs.

Please join me in congratulating Dr. Curtis and Dr. Lindner on their new appointments!

Martin Wynkoop selected as Entrepreneur in Residence

The Engineering Innovation Institute has selected Martin “Marty” Wynkoop, a medical business consultant and former president and CEO of Orthohelix Surgical Designs, as the 2015-2016 Entrepreneur in Residence with a specialization in Medical Technology/Devices. ChemE & EES Lead the College in Safety Improvements

The college continues to make great strides in safety performance improvement. In the past fiscal year, the college has reduced its EHS deficiency findings by 55%. Chemical Engineering and Environmental Engineering Sciences have shown the greatest improvement by decreasing their findings by 89% and 83% respectively.

University Relations wants your research
in the news!

Please email our MarCom team (marcom@eng.ufl.edu) if you are published in these new media channels:

The Conversation

The Conversation is an independent news channel that UF is collaborating with to help elevate the university to the national stage. Their editors work directly with academics to create articles that are then republished in major news outlets. Contributors have the final say on content and headlines. Several faculty from the college have published this summer, and you are invited to as well. Chime in on hot media topics and help set the news agenda with your own original research.

The Chronicle of Higher Education

The Chronicle is published online every weekday. Audited traffic is more than 12.8 million pages a month, seen by more than 1.9 million unique visitors. Successful pitches generally show how institutions are working to overcome challenges, no matter if the result is successful or a work in progress. There are several ways you can contribute:

The Academic Minute

The Academic Minute is a short syndicated public radio recording that features researchers from colleges and universities around the world. It is posted each day to Inside Higher Ed. The producers at The Academic Minute are eager to receive two segments a month from UF...

Media Training

UF’s media experts have designed a training session for faculty who are likely to interact with journalists. This one-hour session is led by media professionals with years of experience on both sides of the camera.

Participants will learn the basics of what to do (and not do) during an interview, how to create talking points and how keep an interview headed in the right direction. The session, conducted in the College of Journalism & Communications studios at Weimer Hall, includes an actual on-camera interview followed by a critique and debriefing.
