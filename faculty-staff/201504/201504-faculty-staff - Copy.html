Associate Dean for Academic Affairs

Dr. Wesley Bolch began his term as our associate dean for academic affairs this January. Dr. Bolch is a professor in the J. Crayton Pruitt Family Department of Biomedical Engineering, affiliate faculty in the Nuclear Engineering Program and he previously served as interim associate dean. He earned his B.S.E. degree in environmental engineering in 1984, and his M.E. and Ph.D. degrees in radiological physics in 1986 and 1998, respectively, all from the University of Florida.

Dr. Bolch is certified by the American Board of Health Physics and was licensed in Radiological Health Engineering by the Texas Board of Professional Engineers in 1992. He is a Fellow of both the Health Physics Society (HPS) and the American Association of Physicists in Medicine (AAPM). He received the 2014 Distinguished Scientific Achievement Award by the Health Physics Society acknowledging outstanding contributions to the science and technology of radiation safety. Welcome aboard, Dr. Bolch!

ISE Interim Chair

Dr. Yongpei Guan has agreed to serve as the interim chair of the Department of Industrial & Systems Engineering while the national search for a permanent chair continues. Dr. Guan is an associate professor who earned his Ph.D. from Georgia Tech in 2005. Before that, he received an MPhil degree from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology and a dual B.S. degree from Shanghai Jiao Tong University. Dr. Guan is the director of UF’s Computational Stochastic Optimization Lab. He won the NSF CAREER award in 2008 and a Young Investigator Award from the Office of Naval Research in 2010. He is currently the associate editor for Journal of Global Optimization and Computational Optimization and Applications, as well as the newsletter editor for the INFORMS Computing Society. He served as the chair for 2014 IIE Annual Conference ISERC Program and was the 2013 Guest Editor-in-Chief for the special issue on “Optimization Methods and Algorithms Applied to Smart Grid” for IEEE Transactions on Smart Grid. He is a recognized expert in stochastic and discrete optimization, supply chain management, and renewable energy integration and power system operations.

LAB SAFETY NEWS

New Initiative: CONNECT

The COE Laboratory Safety office has launched a new initiative to CONNECT with the research labs. CONNECT is an acronym which stands for: Check-In, Offer help, get to kNow the labs, introduce New programs, Evaluate hazards, Correct concerns (non-punitive), and Tout (identify and recognize/reward best practices).

In order to meet these objectives, we plan to visit each research lab 2-4 times per year. Our aim is to promote a positive safety culture by creating a strong relationship with researchers, so that the safety office feels like an extension of every lab – a resource that can focus on finding safety solutions. To set up a CONNECT visit, contact Amy Haberman.

IN MEMORIAM

Dr. Robert “Bob” Ramey
(Dec. 14, 1918 – Mar 7, 2015)

Many friends and colleagues remember Bob and his wife Millie by the distinctive round house the two designed and built on the northwest edge of Gainesville. Before his retirement from UF in 1982, Robert “Bob” Ramey was an associate dean of UF College of Engineering, a professor of electrical engineering, as well as computer and information science, the founding chairman of the Computer and Information Science Department, and associate director of the Engineering & Industrial Experiment Station. Read more »

Dr. Robert Dean
(Nov. 1, 1930 - Feb. 28, 2015)

Professor Emeritus Robert George Dean was known around the world for his coastal engineering research and beach nourishment techniques. He and his wife started the Bob & Phillis Dean Fellowship in Coastal Engineering through the UF Foundation to help support the academic growth of coastal and oceanographic engineering students. Read more »

  

Dr. Kevin Powers
(Dec. 7, 1951 – Jan. 15, 2015)

Dr. Powers was the site director of the National Science Foundation’s Center for Particulate and Surfactant Systems (CPaSS) at UF, and the associate director for the R&D facilities at UF’s Particle Engineering Research Center (PERC). He also taught particle technology, thermodynamics, and engineering design and was a coach for Integrated Process and Product Design program (IPPD) student teams in the College of Engineering.
