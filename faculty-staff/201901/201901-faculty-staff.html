FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Chelsey Simmons (MAE) won a Maximizing Investigators’ Research Award from NIH for Model Systems of Mammalian Regeneration. This $1.8M single investigator award funds a suite of projects focused on mechanobiology of the African Spiny Mouse, the only known regenerative mammal. Its unique cellular response to mechanical stimuli may hold the key to scar-free regeneration after wide-ranging injuries including burns, heart attacks, and spinal cord injury.

Cherie Stabler (BME) received a $1.05M grant from the Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation (JDRF) for a collaborative grant with Andres García (Georgia Tech) entitled “Engineered Bioactive Hydrogel Macro-devices for Islet Transplantation.” Stabler and García will continue their work on developing an immuno-protective device using a unique combination of bioactive biomaterials to protect and support the foreign transplant. The results of their research could provide an ideal implant that would maintain normal glucose levels in a diabetic patient.

ENGINEERS IN THE HEADLINES

Taming the Data Monster to Make Better Decisions

Daisy Zhe Wang, CISE, is bringing order out of chaos with $1M+ support from DARPA. Her collaborative research aims to shorten the time it takes intelligence analysts to collect and interpret data about national and international events.

Using Technology to Stop Deadly Fake Pills

Swarup Bhunia, ECE, and his FICS Research team have developed a breakthrough technology that instantly detects counterfeit pills, a growing problem from developing countries to mature online economies.

Teaming Up To Fight Phishing

Daniela Oliveira, ECE and FICS Research, is teaming up with UF psychologist Natalie Ebner to protect seniors, the most vulnerable age group, from online phishing scams.

UF Develops New Models to Treat Macular Degeneration

Wesley Bolch, BME, developed a rapid and clinically accessible computational eye model that is being explored for possible use in radiotherapy treatment simulation.

Smartphone Security Risk Compared to “Having a Ghost User on Your Phone”

Millions of smartphones likely have vulnerabilities that could allow hackers to easily take control of phones and extract private information without users ever knowing. Kevin Butler, CISE, led a FICS Research team to uncover these “ghostly” weaknesses.

Training the Mind to Balance the Body

Daniel Ferris, BME, is examining the neural mechanisms for human balance and motor coordination. He is studying how the brain responds to different types of visual perturbations using virtual reality. The study will impact how rehabilitation and treatment of various movement disorders and physical disabilities may be improved.
