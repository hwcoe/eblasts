UF Engineer Brings Changes to Florida’s K-12 STEM Education

Early assessments of the Engaging Quality Instruction through Professional Development (EQuIPD) program are revealing its impressive impact among Florida teachers. The EQuIPD program, led by Nancy Ruzycki, Ph.D., MSE, is funded through a $5 million Supporting Effective Educator Development (SEED) grant and specially designed to support teachers in learning new instruction techniques. A program evaluation survey found that teachers with EQuIPD training are implementing more technology and computational thinking in their classrooms and are also using real-world problems to help develop workforce skills in their students.

UF Researchers Find Viable Viruses in Aerosols that Cause COVID-19

New research from the University of Florida provides strong evidence that aerosol transmission of SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19, may be possible. A team of UF researchers, led by John Lednicky, Ph.D., PHHP, and Chang-Yu Wu, Ph.D., ESSIE, found that SARS-CoV-2 in aerosols can be cultured, sparking the need to reevaluate current safety protocols to reduce transmission in indoor spaces.

UF Innovation Station Sarasota County and UF STRIDE Introduce Sarasota County Students to Transportation Engineering

The Southeastern Transportation Research, Innovation, Development, and Education (STRIDE) Center teamed up with Julee Breehne, K-12 Coordinator at Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering’s UF Innovation Station in Sarasota County, to introduce students at Booker Middle School in Sarasota County to transportation engineering. UF Engineer Discovers Astronomical Patterns Can Predict Sea-Level Rise

A newly discovered pattern in sunny-day flooding related to sea level rise could help coastal communities predict and plan for future high-water events. By comparing a century of sea-level data with records of solar and lunar activity also identified in tree rings, scientists at the University of Florida found a link between sunspots, the moon’s orbit and high sea levels in the Gulf of Mexico and the U.S. Atlantic seaboard.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

UF Engineer Receives NSF Grant to Enable Real-Time Surveillance of Antimicrobial Resistance

Christina Boucher, Ph.D., CISE, has received a $1.2 million grant from the NSF. The grant will give Dr. Boucher and her team the opportunity to develop a set of algorithms and an electronic interface that will allow public health investigators to test and analyze biological samples for antibiotic resistance in rural areas.

UF/IFAS Scientists to Shed Light on Aquatic Symbiosis With a Squid

A team of researchers has received a $550,000 grant from the Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation to study the aquatic symbiotic properties in the Hawaiian bobtail squid. Jamie Foster, Ph.D., principal investigator and UF/IFAS professor in microbiology and cell science, is spearheading the project with Carlos Rinaldi, Ph.D., ChE, and David P. Arnold, Ph.D., ECE.

Gahrooei Receives NSF Grant for UFTI Project

Mostafa Reisi Gahrooei, Ph.D., ESSIE, has received a $247K NSF grant for a project entitled "A Dynamic Disruption Prediction System for Transportation Networks at a Road-Segment Level of Granularity." An analytical, data-driven method will be created to monitor and predict disruptions/gridlocks, especially at the granular road-level, to support emergency management systems.

Hartig Leads UF Team in Groundbreaking Multidisciplinary Research Alliance

Kyle Hartig, Ph.D., MSE, will lead a research team as part of the newly formed Interaction of Ionizing Radiation with Matter – University Research Alliance (IIRM-URA). The alliance is funded by a $51.5 million Defense Threat Reduction Agency (DTRA) award. With a diverse coalition of 12 nationally recognized universities and 10 industry affiliates and national labs, the alliance will focus on identifying, understanding and exploiting the fundamental physics of interactions of ionizing radiation with matter. Penn State, the University of Florida, the University of Michigan and the Massachusetts Institute of Technology are the partnership’s four permanent members.

University of Florida Researchers Win Grant to Detect Red Tide Toxins in Air

The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission awarded a $200,000 grant to Myoseon Jang, Ph.D., ESSIE, and Dail Laughinghouse, Ph.D., IFAS, to develop a device to measure how much brevetoxin, the neurotoxic compound found in red tide, is in the air during a bloom and detect how long it survives.

UF Engineer is Using $11M Research Award to Make Steel Manufacturing More Energy Efficient

Backed by a nearly $11 million award from the Department of Energy, the Office of Energy Efficiency and Renewable Energy’s Advanced Manufacturing Office, Michele Manuel, Ph.D., MSE professor and chair, is leading an industry-national laboratory-university consortium in developing an Induction-coupled Thermomagnetic Processing (ITMP) method to help increase the efficiency of alloy manufacturing.

New NSF Award in Cyberinfrastructure for Sustained Scientific Innovation (CSSI): Elements

Janise McNair, Ph.D., ECE, is beginning a new multi-discipline collaboration with Sanjeev Koppal, Ph.D., ECE, and researchers in the College of Design, Construction, and Planning. This project will develop a robust cyberinfrastructure (CI) system and service for construction research and applications to address challenges faced in the construction industry

UF Health Researchers Look to Smartwatch App to Fend Off Pandemic Face Touching

Mamoun Mardini, Ph.D., BME, a data scientist who is affiliated with the UF Institute on Aging, and co-PI Lisa Anthony, Ph.D., CISE, received a $20,000, six-month pilot grant from the UF Informatics Institute for their effort to build a smartwatch app that will notify wearers every time their hand touches their face, essentially training wearers to avoid touching their faces and thus reduce the spread of COVID-19.

Major Grant Launches New Project to Advance Global Soil Science
