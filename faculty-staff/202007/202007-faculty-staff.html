JULY 2020

COVID-19: UF'S PLAN FORWARD

The State University System of Florida Board of Governors has approved the reopening plan for the University of Florida. UF's plan outlines how the university will strive to create a safer, healthier campus environment and continue its research and education missions through screening, testing and enhanced protective measures.

The plan will continue to be updated as circumstances with COVID-19 evolve both globally and locally. You can learn more and share your thoughts by visiting the "Our Plan Forward" section of UF's website.

New Engineers Speak Up at Graduation Celebration

Three students graduating this spring from the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering spoke at the college’s 2020 online graduation celebration, held on May 3. Jayla Bradley and Sylvester Kwo represented the students receiving their Bachelor of Science degrees in Engineering, while Yao Xiao spoke on behalf of those students receiving their Ph.D. degrees.


ELI and EII Soar Past Goals for Academic Year 2019-2020

The Engineering Innovation Institute made a 22% increase in student enrollment from last year, comprising 642 undergraduate and 192 graduate students. In addition, this past year saw 2,160 unique combined student enrollments (Innovation and Leadership Institutes) in the curricula offered, continuing a ~30% annual enrollment growth rate for the two Institutes since inception. The growth has exceeded the Fiscal Year 2023 projected target goal of 2,000 unique enrollments set as part of the Wertheim Transformation initiative, marking a significant milestone for the institutes.

University of Florida and Sarasota County Renew Innovation Station Funding

Sarasota County commissioners have unanimously approved extending funding, matched by University of Florida funding, for the UF Innovation Station Sarasota County (UFIS-SC) through 2027. UFIS-SC was formed in 2016 and is the first extension office for the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering. It was developed to strengthen the region’s innovation economy, support the growth of high-tech, high-wage jobs, increase technology transfer and employee retention and foster start-up opportunities in the Sarasota area.

Wind Engineering Laboratory Makes Economy Face Shields to Support UF Anesthesiology

The Case for Building Hazard-Resilient Residential Communities

On March 3, a tornado touched down in Nashville, Tenn., resulting in over 20 fatalities and the destruction of dozens of buildings. David O. Prevatt, Ph.D., an associate professor in the Engineering School of Sustainable Infrastructure and Environment (ESSIE) who was deployed to Nashville to conduct a post-tornado damage investigation, continues to strongly advocate for adherence to hazard-resilient building codes and guidelines.

FACULTY SUCCESS

Koppal Receives NSF CAREER Award for Active Computer Vision

Sanjeev Koppal, Ph.D., ECE, has received an NSF CAREER Award for his project “Fast Foveation: Bringing Active Vision into the Camera.” The five-year award will support Dr. Koppal’s research in the area of foveation, the process through which the eye fixates on the object of interest in a visual field.

UF Engineering takes home seven National Science Foundation CAREER Awards

Each year, the National Science Foundation bestows its most prestigious awards “in support of early-career faculty who have the potential to serve as academic role models in research and education and to lead advances in the mission of their department or organization.” In the 2019-2020 academic year, Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering faculty applied for 18 NSF CAREER Awards. From those applications, seven awards were made to early career faculty in the engineering college, a strong win for UF.

Novel Peer-to-Peer Car Charging System Proposes to Automate, Mobilize EV Charging

A novel system proposed by Swarup Bhunia, Ph.D., ECE, and researchers at the Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World aims to help make electric vehicles a practical means of long-distance travel in the United States. The system will combine cloud computing, 5G connectivity, machine learning, and automotive automation systems to create what they call Peer-to-Peer Car Charging.

$15M Technology Transition Center Celebrates Virtual Kickoff

Gunduz inducted into prestigious UF Graduate Honor Society

Aysegul Gunduz, Ph.D., BME, associate professor, has been selected for induction into the 2020 class of the UF Graduate School Edward Alexander Bouchet Graduate Honor Society. The Bouchet Society, named for the first African-American to earn a Ph.D. in the United States (in physics at Yale in 1876), promotes diversity in higher education by recognizing noteworthy achievement and advocacy for students who have been traditionally underrepresented in the academia.

Gilbert Elected as Member of the Academy of Science, Engineering and Medicine of Florida

Juan E. Gilbert, Ph.D., CISE, The Banks Family Preeminence Endowed Professor and department chair, was recently elected as a member of the Academy of Science, Engineering and Medicine of Florida (ASEMFL). Dr. Gilbert was elected for his pioneering effort in human-centered computing research that focused on societal issues and his national leadership in advancing diversity in higher education.

Moore Earns KL2 Career Development Award

Erika Moore, Ph.D., MSE, holder of the Rhines Rising Star Larry Hench professorship, has been awarded the UF Clinical and Translational Science Institute (CTSI) KL2 Career Development Award.

UF MSE Researchers Discover Potential Pathway for Bone Growth

A team of researchers led by Josephine Allen, Ph.D., MSE, have developed an injectable composite that spontaneously forms into nanofibers resembling the microscopic structure of the human body and could ultimately be custom designed for an infinite number of biomedical applications including connective-tissue repair, drug delivery, wound healing or skin care.

Tabrizian Selected As Hastings Faculty Fellow

In recognition of his superior work in the area of radio frequency micro- and nano-electro-mechanical resonators and transducers, Roozbeh Tabrizian, Ph.D., ECE, was announced as the Alan Hastings Faculty Fellow for 2020-2023. The Alan Hastings Faculty Fellow was created in 2017 with a generous gift from ECE alumnus Alan Hastings (BSEE ’85).

Three Engineering Faculty Named UF Research Foundation Professors

Richard Hennig, Ph.D., MSE, Prabhat Mishra, Ph.D., CISE, and Ghatu Subhash, Ph.D., MAE have been named as UFRF Professors for 2020. The recognition goes to faculty who have a distinguished current record of research and a strong research agenda that is likely to lead to continuing distinction in their fields.

UNIVERSITY NEWS

UF is First U.S. University to Acquire NVIDIA DGX A100 System, Advancing AI University Initiative

Moving forward on its vision to transform the future of education through artificial intelligence (AI), the University of Florida is collaborating with technology company NVIDIA to acquire the world’s most advanced AI system to boost the performance of UF’s powerful supercomputer. In addition, the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering will provide the programmatic backbone for UF’s AI University Initiative. The college plans to create the necessary curricular resources so that Gator graduates from every discipline will be empowered with an exceptional level of digital literacy and equipped to apply AI techniques in their chosen field of study.

At the same time, the university has made tremendous infrastructure investments, such as the new $150M Data Science and Information Technology (DSIT) Building that will house and unify campus research on biomedical informatics and health outcomes and applied research programs at the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering. The Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World and the Florida Institute for Cybersecurity Research (FICS Research), along with CISE and ECE, will also call the new DSIT Building home.

UF Ranked No. 1 Nationally in Innovation Impact

The University of Florida is the most productive large university in the country at leveraging its research funding into new companies, new jobs and new ideas, according to a report released last week by the George W. Bush Institute and the Opus Faveo Innovation Development consulting firm.

UF Mobilizes Campus, Exactech Expertise to Make Vital COVID-19 Nasal Swabs

COVID-19 Research Roundup

Gator engineers are shaping the future of things that are vital to society as we traverse the COVID-19 pandemic landscape: reliable testing, contact tracing methods, protection for healthcare workers, predictive models for efficient healthcare operations, and many other necessities.

Bhunia and Ray Collaborate with Northrop Grumman on DARPA Contract

Swarup Bhunia, Ph.D., and Co-PI Sandip Ray, Ph.D., both ECE, won a $2M DARPA contract with Northrop Grumman Corporation to develop a secure system-on-chip (SoC). The SoC serves as the central processing unit in applications including Internet of Things devices such as smart thermostats and smart wearables. The award will enable development of a comprehensive platform, consisting of computer-aided design (CAD) tools, architecture and metrics, which will automate the design of secure computing systems.

UF Engineers Develop HVAC Powered by Multiple Energy Sources

Saeed Moghaddam, Ph.D., Knox T. Millsaps Professor, MAE, along with industry and organizational partners, has received a $1.89M grant from the U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) to develop a breakthrough cooling and heating system.

NSF Awards Phase II Funding to MIST Center

Figueiredo, NSF Collaborate to Apply Edge Computing to Lake Ecology Forecasting
