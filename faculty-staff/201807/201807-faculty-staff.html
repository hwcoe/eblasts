COLLEGE NEWS

NSF Center for Big Learning Kicks Off

The NSF Center for Big Learning hosted a two-day kickoff meeting with affiliated faculty from partnering universities, government representatives and industry partners. The Center, led by Andy Li, ECE, was awarded by NSF as a Phase I IUCRC Center for five years with a vision to continue innovation in an intelligence-powered society.

New Director of IPPD

Integrated Product and Process Design, a senior capstone program within the Engineering Innovation Institute, has named Rodney Guico, Ph.D., as its new director. Dr. Guico has led several initiatives within the college including the Florida Engineering Experiment Station Network (FLEXNet) and Electronic Delivery of Gator Engineering (EDGE).

New Director of Industry Engagement

Kimberly Jacobs, Ph.D., was appointed as Director of Industry Engagement. In this role, she is responsible for managing strategic relationships between the college and industry stakeholders. Dr. Jacobs served as the Director of Corporate Relations for the college prior to this appointment, where she was pivotal in advancing corporate philanthropy and outreach.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Additional details now available on these two previously reported grants.

Blanka Sharma and Kyle Allen, BME, were awarded $1.57M from NIH's National Institute of Arthritis and Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases (NIAMS) to provide new insights into disease mechanisms and advance new drug delivery technologies that could lead to effective treatments for osteoarthritis.

Daisy Zhe Wang, CISE, is funded by DARPA for a 4.5-year, 1.17M, project to develop a multihypothesis semantic engine that generates explicit alternative interpretations of events, situations, and trends from a variety of unstructured sources, for use in noisy, conflicting, and potentially deceptive information environments. The core technology includes constructing, mining and querying over a probabilistic knowledge graph. This grant is part of a larger collaboration with USC ISI (actual name please?), Columbia University and Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.

ENGINEERS IN THE HEADLINES

NYPD Tests New Tool That Detects Credit Card Skimmers

Patrick Traynor, CISE, and his team at FICS have invented a device called the “Skim Reaper” to easily detect if an ATM or gas pump has been compromised. The New York Police Department is testing the Skim Reaper with some early success in its effort to rid the streets of the pervasive defrauding devices.

The University of Florida Has a World-Class Weather Lab

The Powell Family Structures and Materials Laboratory attracts researchers from across the world. Supported research areas include blast engineering, earthquakes, tornadoes and of course, hurricanes.

Engineering the Human Brain

From Alzheimer’s research to electrical stimulations, UF’s neural engineers are developing new techniques to unravel the complexities of the human brain.

IoT SUMMER SERIES

The summer edition of Explore, a magazine produced by the UF Office of Research, highlights engineering faculty focused on the Internet of Things.

  • Connected World: From tiny sensors to worldwide connections, ECE faculty are at the forefront of IoT research. Features David Arnold, John Harris, Yier Jin, Sandip Ray and Mark Tehranipoor of ECE.
  • Cloud Roots: Toshi Nishida and David Arnold, ECE, explain how the MIST Center helps provide the roots, or hardware technology, for smart systems.
  • iDrive For Traffic Research: Lily Elefteriadou, ESSIE, leads the UF Transportation Institute and I-STREET, a real-world testbed for emerging transportation technologies. One of her initiatives, the self-driving shuttle from UF to Downtown Gainesville, recently hit the streets.
  • Peak Comfort While Saving Energy: Using sensors and IoT, Prabir Barooah, MAE, is testing personal comfort nodes, or PCNs, to optimize energy use for buildings.
  • Precision Navigation: Xiaolin Andy Li, ECE, and his colleagues have just patented an indoor microGPS that works without satellites.
  • Phish in A Barrel: Daniela Oliveira, ECE, is researching the impacts of phishing and aging.
  • Mouth Monitor: Y.K. Yoon, ECE, has developed the first IoT device that can mine data from the human mouth.
  • Forecasting Haiti: Using off-the-shelf components, Bill Eisenstadt, ECE, and his team engineered an affordable solution to bring weather data back to the country.
