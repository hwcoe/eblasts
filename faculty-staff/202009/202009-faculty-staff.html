Gilbert Creates inLine Ticketing System to Lower Health Risk When Voting

Juan E. Gilbert, Ph.D., The Banks Family Preeminence Endowed Professor and chair of the UF Department of Computer & Information Science & Engineering (CISE), has created a ticketing system to help voters maintain social distancing while exercising their right to vote. After identifying the concerns of sending thousands to the polls during a pandemic, Dr. Gilbert started working on an easy-to-use system that allows poll workers to hand out tickets to voters waiting in line. These tickets are printed out on an as-needed-basis and given to voters as the lines get long. Each ticket is printed with a QR code, along with a date and time to return for voting in English and Spanish. As voters return at their designated time, the QR code is scanned, and they proceed to vote.

Kaplan Writes Brief to U.S. Supreme Court Regarding Clean Water Act

David Kaplan, Ph.D., ESSIE, along with a team of scientists, wrote an amicus curiae brief for the U.S. Supreme Court about a case regarding the Clean Water Act (CWA) that will have a lasting impact on public policy. In April, the Supreme Court declared, in a 6-3 ruling, that the CWA requires the federal government to regulate some groundwater pollutants that can navigate into waters such as oceans, rivers and streams.

“The most exciting thing about being involved in this process was to see the role of science validated in setting public policy, especially regarding the environment. Seeing the justices read, consider and even cite our brief in their decision has been one of the proudest moments of my career,” Dr. Kaplan said.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Boucher Receives NSF Grant

Christina Boucher, Ph.D., CISE, is leading a new project funded by NSF Smart and Connected Health entitled “Enabling Real-Time Surveillance of Antimicrobial Resistance.” The goal of this project is to use succinct data structures and machine learning to create mobile bioinformatics methods for on-site, real-time detection of antimicrobial resistance (AMR) using miniaturized sequencing nanopore technology. The methods will work on-device, meaning they will only use the hardware (RAM, cache, hard disk, processors) on the portable device, which will allow them to detect AMR in remote areas.

Du Receives NSF RAPID Award to Measure New Normal Post COVID-19

Eric Jing Du, Ph.D., ESSIE, plans to examine and predict changes to people’s mobility behavior with a Rapid Response Research (RAPID) Award from the National Science Foundation. Dr. Du is collaborating with Ryan Qi Wang, Ph.D., at Northeastern University. The ultimate goal is to enable improved citizens’ preparation and public agencies’ responses to future public health crises.

FICS Research Receives $7.8M to Help Make On-Chip Security Pervasive

Florida Institute for Cybersecurity (FICS) Research has announced a collaboration with the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) on a program called Automated Implementation of Secure Silicon (AISS). The grant has the broad goal of making scalable on-chip security pervasive throughout industry and military applications.

Gardner-McCune Working on National Effort to Define K-12 AI Curriculum

Christina Gardner-McCune, Ph.D., CISE, is working on a project to develop an artificial intelligence (AI) curriculum for grades K-12. Dr. Gardner-McCune is co-chair of the AI for K-12 Initiative (AI4K12), which was recently featured in the NSF-funded 2020 STEM for All Video Showcase. The project was established to determine what students in each grade should know about artificial intelligence, machine learning, and robotics.

Jain Awarded Grant to Develop a CRISPR-based Urine Test for Early Detection of Breast Cancer

Piyush Jain, Ph.D., ChE, has been awarded a grant from the Florida Breast Cancer Foundation to develop a CRISPR-based urine test for early detection of breast cancer. Similar to a pregnancy strip, the paper-based test would provide a quick and easy detection method without requiring a hospital, lab, or instrumentation, and could be used to diagnose other types of cancer and genetic diseases at an early stage.

MAE Researchers Receive $1.01M Contract to Operate Autonomous Vehicles Lab at REEF Facility

David Jeffcoat, Ph.D., MAE, and Prashant Ganesh, Ph.D., MAE, won a $1.01M contract with Air Force Research Lab (AFRL) to operate an Autonomous Vehicles Lab (AVL) at the UF Research and Engineering Education Facility (REEF) in Shalimar, Florida. The AVL is used by a team of UF faculty, staff, and students, along with AFRL researchers, for theoretical development and hardware implementation of single agent and multi-agent navigation algorithms.

Jiang Awarded a 2020 Space Research Institute Grant

Peng Jiang, Ph.D., ChE, was awarded a grant from the Space Research Initiative (SRI seed fund) to develop a colorimetric shear stress sensor for aerodynamic measurements. The Jiang Lab will design, produce, and test a color-changing stress sensor that provides easy measurements of friction forces in an aerodynamic environment.

Kim Receives Grant from U.S. Department of State

Gloria Kim, Ph.D., EED, was awarded a new grant entitled “U.S.-Korea Global Design Thinking Alliance for Community College Students.” This project will address low study abroad participation of STEM students through the creation of a new faculty-led study-abroad program in South Korea. This 12-week immersive summer program is a design thinking course for students in the Gator Engineering at Santa Fe program, taught by UF faculty.

Xian Receives Funding to Develop Model to Administer Mass COVID-19 Testing

Xiaochen Xian, Ph.D., ISE, recently received funding from the NSF in support of her research to develop adaptive sampling strategies for administering mass testing for COVID-19. The purpose of the research is to establish a reliable framework that can identify patterns of virus transmission in vulnerable communities while minimizing the consumption of testing resources, such as the tests themselves, materials that make the tests, testing toolkits, testing personnel time and effort.
