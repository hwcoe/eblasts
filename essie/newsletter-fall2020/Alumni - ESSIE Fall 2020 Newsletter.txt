UF ESSIE: Fall 2020 Newsletter

MESSAGE FROM THE DIRECTOR

Welcome to the Engineering School of Sustainable Infrastructure & Environment (ESSIE) at the University of Florida's digital newsletter. Each semester, you will receive an email from us highlighting School updates and successes. We are pleased to share with you our Fall 2020 digital newsletter. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/fall-2020-letter-from-the-director

Kirk Hatfield, Ph.D.
Director
Engineering School of Sustainable Infrastructure & Environment

NEW FACULTY HIRES
Meet the newest faculty members in ESSIE. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/2020-new-faculty-hires

RESEARCH SPOTLIGHT
Eric Jing Du, Ph.D., plans to examine and predict changes to people’s mobility behavior since the COVID-19 pandemic with a RAPID Award from the National Science Foundation. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/measuring-the-new-normal-post-covid-19

RESEARCH SPOTLIGHT
A team of researchers, led by Chang-Yu Wu, Ph.D., provides strong evidence that aerosol transmission of SARS-CoV-2, the virus that causes COVID-19, may be possible. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/uf-researchers-find-viable-viruses-in-aerosols-that-cause-covid-19

RESEARCH SPOTLIGHT
Katherine Deliz Quiñones, Ph.D., received support from the Environmental Protection Agency’s STAR program to help build resilience in vulnerable communities. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/deliz-receives-epa-grant-to-build-community-resilience-against-pfas-exposure-due-to-flooding

STUDENT SPOTLIGHT
Sydney Williams was accepted to the Margaret A. Davidson Graduate Fellowship Program and received funding by the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration’s Office for Coastal Management. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/doctoral-student-named-margaret-a-davidson-fellow

SOMETHING’S IN THE WATER
David Kaplan, Ph.D., wrote an amicus curiae brief for the U.S. Supreme Court about a case regarding the Clean Water Act that will have a lasting impact on public policy. Read more:  https://www.essie.ufl.edu/somethings-in-the-water

ALUMNI SPOTLIGHT
Sergio Galeano established a fund to support environmental engineering students in perpetuity with a planned gift. Read more: https://www.essie.ufl.edu/their-fathers-footsteps
