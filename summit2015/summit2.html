Keynote Speaker

Kim Stevenson
Corporate Vice President and CIO, Intel Corporation
As CIO of Intel, Stevenson leads a diverse team of technologists—over 6,000 worldwide—within a company that is known for being the paragon of technology innovation. Since she took the position, her use of big data analytics has sped up product supply and improved employee productivity.

Speakers

Linda Hudson
CEO, The Cardea Group
(BS SE ’72) Distinguished as the first female CEO in the defense industry, Hudson ran the global aerospace company BAE Systems after a long string of leadership positions at Harris Corporation, Ford Aerospace, Martin Marietta, and General Dynamics.

Brigadier General
Maureen LeBoeuf, Ed.D.
A retired helicopter pilot, LeBoeuf became the first woman to run a department—the Department of Physical Education—at the United States Military Academy at West Point. Her position carried the unique title “Master of the Sword.”

Marie Tillman
President, Pat Tillman Foundation
After her husband died in Afghanistan, Tillman created a foundation that in the last 10 years has invested more than $10M into educational support for people committed to service.
