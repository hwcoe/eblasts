Dear %%First Name%%,
When you make the choice to sponsor a student’s graduation pin within the J. Crayton Pruitt Family Department of Biomedical Engineering, you are joining a special annual tradition of supporting the next generation of engineers.
“The pinning ceremony is much more than a simple token of graduation: It’s a pact or a promise to uphold. People don’t become biomedical engineers for notoriety, money, or fame: They do it because they want to give back. The legacy we leave behind is not just a new drug, prosthetic, or imaging system: it’s helping someone walk again; it’s helping someone smile again; it’s helping someone get their life back; it’s the betterment of the human condition. The pinning ceremony reminds us where we’ve been and shows us our responsibility in the future as lifelong students, Gators, and humans.”
– Aaron Johnston, Class of 2016
This graduation pin is a representation that will stay with the Class of 2017 long after they walk across the stage. This pin will be a reminder and foundational symbol for the rest of their lives. You have the extraordinary opportunity to help each student receive their unique BME pin with a simple $100 gift.
“It is an honor and a privilege to participate in this BME tradition. We hope that providing a small token of remembrance of this day will set a positive example for the recipients to remember to "give back" to the department as they make their way in this exciting biomedical engineering world.”
– Gary & Suzy Miller, BME Pin Donors
Each student will receive a custom designed pin and tailored notecard with the name of their pin donor, along with a personalized message you send to us for the student: a great way to meaningfully connect with your sponsored student!
Sponsor a Pin
New for 2017! Any BME alum who graduated in the last five years may participate with a pin sponsorship donation of only $50. Your participation is especially important as we look to grow this annual tradition with you!
All supporters are invited to attend the 4th Annual Pinning Ceremony on Sunday, April 30th, 2017 at 3:00 p.m. in the Reitz Union Rion Ballroom.
Your response is requested by Friday, April 14th.

Thank you!
