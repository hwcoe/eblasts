April 12 - Seminar: Security and Privacy Challenges in the Internet of Things
Join us for an IoT seminar by Dr. Yier Jin: Security and Privacy Challenges in the Internet of Things
Presented by
The Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World
Wednesday, April 12, 2017 • 2:30–3:30pm
Reitz Union Chamber • Ground level, located at the bottom of the Atrium across from the Wing Zone at the Orange and Brew
Reception to follow
University of Florida professor Yier Jin made headlines in 2014 when he demonstrated that hackers could use "smart" thermostats to spy on homeowners. His seminar will outline plans for a more secure, connected future, including current efforts at the Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World to systematically summarize IoT vulnerabilities and to design trusted protocols for secure IoT systems.
Learn more

Yier Jin is an endowed term professor and the first faculty hired at the new Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World. His research focuses on embedded systems design and security, trusted hardware intellectual property cores, and hardware-software co-design for modern computing systems. He received his Ph.D. in electrical engineering from Yale University.
