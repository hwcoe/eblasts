Seminar: Security and Privacy Challenges in the Internet of Things
Featuring Dr. Yier Jin, Endowed Term Professor in the Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World, Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering
Location: Reitz Union Room 3315
Date: April 12, 2017
Time: 2:30–3:30pm
Reception to follow
The number of IoT devices in the world is approaching 15 billion – roughly two per human, and nearly all produced in the last decade. By 2020 that number will reach 50 billion. With this fast and furious growth and deployment of connected devices, security and privacy concerns have too frequently become an afterthought. A compromised device presents serious security threats. It can be used to extract sensitive information, attack other devices, or send malware over the Internet.
Join Yier Jin, Ph.D., from the University of Florida’s Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World as he outlines plans for a more secure, connected future, including current efforts to systematically summarize IoT vulnerabilities and to design trusted protocols for secure IoT systems.

Yier Jin, Ph.D.

Yier Jin is the first hire at the new Warren B. Nelms Institute for the Connected World. He will officially join the faculty at the University of Florida on July 1, 2017 as an endowed term professor. He received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from Yale University after earning his B.S. and M.S. degrees in Electrical Engineering from Zhejiang University in China. His research focuses on the areas of embedded systems design and security, trusted hardware intellectual property cores, and hardware-software co-design for modern computing systems. His is currently focusing on the design and security analysis of Internet of Things (IoT) and wearable devices, with particular emphasis on information integrity and privacy protection in the IoT era. Dr. Jin was guest editor of IEEE’s 52nd Design Automation Conference in 2015 and its 21st Asia and South Pacific Design Automation Conference in 2016.
