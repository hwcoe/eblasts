Message from Dean Abernathy: Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering’s Response to COVID-19

Dear colleagues and friends of the college,

I hope that you and your families are safely weathering COVID-19. The health and economic conditions brought on by this global pandemic are affecting many of us in ways we have not experienced before, but I am certain that we will handle this crisis as Gators always do, with innovation, creativity and resilience.

I would like to take a moment to update you on our current status and policies we have put in place to tackle the challenges as we keep our business in operation.

  • The Alachua County Commission ordered all non-essential businesses to close and residents to stay at home, starting at midnight on Monday, March 23. Gainesville mayor Lauren Poe mirrored those orders for the city.
  • Currently, UF remains open, but is limited to on-campus essential services, including maintaining the safety and wellbeing of our students, faculty and staff, as well as integrity of infrastructure, property and systems on-site. All other staff are working remotely, and supervisors and team leaders continue to monitor progress and ensure deliverables.
  • Faculty, researchers and staff who are responsible for laboratories and research work are following these guidelines to ensure safety and continuity:
    • Most faculty and research personnel are working from home, including doctoral students serving as research assistants. Faculty are able to access their labs, but have been encouraged to limit or wind down experiments.
    • Long standing experiments and critical facilities are still being maintained with applicable personnel and facilities access.
    • For all lab and office environments, social distancing of at least 6 feet is a requirement.
    • Meetings are being conducted via Zoom or other virtual meeting tools.
  • For students, online instruction has been implemented to help ensure our students continue on their educational path.
    • Spring commencement has been rescheduled for the weekend of July 31-August 2 for students graduating with master or bachelor degrees. Graduating doctoral students can participate in the August 7 summer doctoral ceremony.
    • UF’s virtual library is open with help available for students, and academic advisors are meeting with students via Zoom.
    • Student housing is currently [XXXXX, waiting for UF Central to confirm status] and UF dining locations are supplying to-go meals only.

This crisis is incredibly disruptive to the research and education missions of the college, but we are committed to continue our stride in ways that Gator Engineers know how—safely, collaboratively and creatively. I can’t thank you enough for your patience and your understanding, and I especially want to say thank you to all of our faculty and staff for making it possible for us to continue to provide a safe and productive working and education environment for our college during this difficult time.

Stay safe and healthy,

Cammy R. Abernathy
Dean, Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering
