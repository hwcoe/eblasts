COLLEGE NEWS

UF Honored with ASEE Engineering Excellence for Veterans Award

HWCOE has been recognized with a 2018 Engineering Excellence for Veterans Award from the American Society of Engineering Education (ASEE). This award is given to recognize academic institutions that are leaders in helping veterans bridge the gap between military and civilian worlds.

UF to House the First NSF Center for Big Learning

After over two years of development across academic institutions and industry partners, the NSF Center for Big Learning (CBL) has been officially awarded to UF. As the first NSF center for big learning, the CBL will be an NSF Industry & University Cooperative Research Center (I/UCRC) and will be housed on the fourth floor of the New Engineering Building (NEB).

UF joins MetroLab Network With the City of Gainesville

The collaboration will focus on three research initiatives: 1) an autonomous shuttle between campus and downtown Gainesville; 2) finding better ways to connect patients with local healthcare systems; and 3) a smart street lighting project with sensor technology.

UF Participates in USA Science & Engineering Festival in Washington DC

CISE students and alumni showcased UF innovations at the nation’s largest gathering of STEM exhibits, shows and hands-on activities.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Jaime Ruiz, CISE, Parisa Rashidi, BME and Roozbeh Tabrisian, ECE, received NSF CAREER Awards. They join more than 20 active CAREER projects at the college.

Daniel Ferris, BME, received a $2M NIH grant to investigate how the human brain controls locomotor adaptation under varying conditions. Information gained from this study could aid in developing new methods of diagnosis and treatment for gait disabilities.

Kyle Allen, BME, received a $1.8M grant from the National Institute of Health to identify and investigate sources of osteoarthritis and conditions of tissue deterioration. The research will focus on looking beyond the articular cartilage to see what changes in which specific tissues are really driving the symptomology of osteoarthritis.

Blanka Sharma and Kyle Allen, BME, were awarded $1.57M from NIH’s National Institute of Arthritis and Musculoskeletal and Skin Diseases (NIAMS) to provide new insights into disease mechanisms and advance new drug delivery technologies that could lead to effective treatments for osteoarthritis.

Daisy Zhe Wang, CISE, was awarded $1.17M from DARPA.
