Virginia “Ginger” Gibson Appointed as Executive Director of Finance

Ms. Gibson received her B.S. degree in Accounting from Central Washington University in 1994, and her M.S. in Accounting from the University of Florida in 2000. She has held a Certified Public Accountant license in Florida since 2001. She has served as Vice Chair and Chair of the Florida College’s Council of Business Affairs and the Florida College System Accounting Committee. She was awarded the 2007 Community College Business Officer International Outstanding Business Officer Award and in 2017 was awarded the Florida College System Ken Jarrett Service Award in recognition for her outstanding contributions to the Florida College System. Ms. Gibson begins her appointment on Nov. 5. The college is pleased to welcome Ginger to the Gator Engineering family.

Bill Heitman, former Executive Director of Finance, retired on Aug. 8. Bill has been in the position for 10 years and has done an outstanding job. He has created a financial management system and fiscal team for the college that is second to none on campus. We thank Bill for his tireless contributions and congratulate him on his retirement.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Wesley Bolch (BME) Awarded $3.2M Collaborative NIH NIBIB Grant

The grant entitled, “MIRDcalc: A Community Tool for Deriving and Reporting Patient Organ Doses in Nuclear Medicine, Computed Tomography and Hybrid Imaging” will develop and release a new generation of nuclear medicine patient dosimetry code at no cost to the imaging and clinical community.

Ruogu Fang (BME) and Mingzhou Ding (BME) to Study Brain Dynamics with NSF Grant

Fang and Ding’s proposal is a three-year research program that seeks to characterize and develop a multi-level connectivity model for brain dynamics and corresponding tools to significantly accelerate data-driven discoveries from dynamic neuroimaging data. The study aims to transform brain dynamics modeling, which could result in the comprehensive identification of neurovascular diseases that could further advance personalized neuroscience.

Christopher Ferraro (ESSIE) Received Funding Through ARPA-E: Innovating Through Unconventional Ideas

The $1.1M grant to explore the topic of extremely durable concretes and cementitious materials, will develop a means to combat the damage that neutron exposure causes to concrete used to house nuclear reactors. The project will explore novel additives incorporating boron and will determine whether or not, when bombarded with neutron radiation, these can produce lithium that improves the concrete’s strength and extends its lifetime.

Greg Hudalla (BME) Received the NIH Outstanding Investigator Award

This $1.8M award will enable Hudalla and colleagues to develop new biomaterials through the self-assembly of peptides modified with sugars.

UF is Home to Air Force Center of Excellence for Hardware Security

The Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering and The Ohio State University College of Engineering are collaborating in two Centers of Excellence sponsored by the Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR), Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), and Nimbis Inc, with a grant value of more than $9 million in the first year.

Karim Oweiss (ECE) Leads New NIH Funded Project in Brain and Movement Disorder Research

The $3.9M award will enable Oweiss and his collaborators to use state of the art deep brain recording and stimulation technology to characterize neural signatures of limb movements in deep brain structures and use these signatures to provide on-demand stimulation therapy in neighboring brain structures to alleviate severe essential tremor symptoms in human patients.

Tom Shrimpton (CISE) Awarded $1.5M Grant on DARPA RACE Effort in Partnership with Portland Tech Company Galois

The Resilient Anonymous Communication for Everyone (RACE) program will research technologies for a distributed messaging system that can exist completely within a given network, provide confidentiality, integrity, and availability of messaging, while preserving privacy to any participant in the system.

Patrick Traynor, Vincent Bindschaedler and Tom Shrimpton (CISE) Receive $1.2M from NSF to Investigate Digital Assistant Security

Patrick Traynor and co-PIs Vincent Bindschaedler and Tom Shrimpton, CISE, won a $1.2M Award from the National Science Foundation entitled “Securing the Voice Processing Pipeline Against Adversarial Audio.” This research investigates how digital assistants (e.g. Alexa and Siri), which rely on voice interfaces, can be exploited and how such systems can be better protected through lightweight signal processing defenses.

UF Researchers Awarded $5M Grant for Type 1 Diabetes Research

Cherie Stabler, BME, and The UF Diabetes Institute have been awarded a $5M grant to continue in the development of a “pancreatic islet on a chip” for diabetes research. The islets play a key role in the production of beta cells which produce insulin for the human body.

UF Engineering Hopes to Curb Harmful Chemicals in Landfills with EPA Grant

Timothy Townsend, Ph.D., ESSIE, is the PI of an interdisciplinary group of collaborators that has received part of a $6M total grant to study per- and poly-fluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) and their role in landfills.
