UF cracks U.S. News List of Top 10 Best Public Universities

The University of Florida has become the first Florida school to break into the list of top 10 best public universities, coming in at No. 9, according to the 2018 U.S. News & World Report Best Colleges rankings.
Read more»

Toshi Nishida Named Interim Associate Dean of Academic Affairs

Toshikazu “Toshi” Nishida, associate chair of research in ECE and affiliate professor in MAE, began his role as Interim Associate Dean for Academic Affairs on July 15, 2017. Nishida serves as director of the NSF MIST Center and is a co-founder of the Interdisciplinary Microsystems Group. As an integral member of the college leadership team, Nishida will oversee academic affairs and graduate programs. The search for a permanent associate dean will begin in September.

Wes Bolch, who previously served in this role, has returned to full-time faculty status and will continue his ongoing research activities in the areas of medical physics and radiological engineering. He is currently working with college leadership to develop a new program in Crisis Leadership Engineering where he will take the lead in the area of radiological response to nuclear and terrorist incidents.

Richard Dickinson Joins National Science Foundation

Richard Dickinson, chair of the Department of Chemical Engineering (ChemE), joined the National Science Foundation as director of the Chemical, Biological, Environmental, and Transport Systems (CBET) Division on August 16, 2017. With 16 programs and a budget of nearly $200 million, the CBET division is an essential source of NSF research funding. As a rotator, Dickinson will retain his UF faculty position and return to campus after his appointment in Washington.

Carlos Rinaldi to Serve as Interim Chair of ChemE

Carlos Rinaldi has agreed to serve as interim chair of ChemE, effective August 1, 2017. Rinaldi is currently the Charles A. Stokes Term Professor in Chemical Engineering, with a joint appointment in BME. His research is focused on advancing the understanding and biomedical applications of suspensions of magnetic nanoparticles. The search for a permanent chair will begin in September.

New Associate Director, Facilities Operations

The college welcomes Steven Vann as the new Associate Director, Facilities Operations. Prior to joining UF, Steven was the Director of Facilities and Asset Management at LifeSouth Community Blood Centers of Gainesville, where he was responsible for the organization’s 30 fixed sites, 47 bloodmobiles, 75 light duty vehicles’ and all of its critical equipment associated with the processing and storing of blood components. Other highlights from his extensive portfolio include completion of 15,000 and 30,000 sq. ft. buildings, selection and management of design and construction teams, contract management and budget and project tracking. Steven graduated from UF with a Bachelor of Science in Zoology in 1997. He replaces Denis Mercier, who will be retiring from UF in 2018 after serving the College for more than two decades.

FACULTY RESEARCH GRANTS

Kevin Otto, BME, and Karim Oweiss, ECE, received $8.4 million from DARPA to study brain training using electric stimulation. They are among eight team leaders across the country receiving awards under the Targeted Neuroplasticity Training program.


Oweiss and Otto were also featured on Reddit’s Science AMA Series.

Benjamin Keselowsky, BME, was co-awarded $2.6 million from the National Institutes of Health to pursue an MS vaccine.

Kristy Boyer, CISE, received a $3 million grant from NSF to foster collaborative computer science learning for upper elementary students.

Jose Fortes, CISE, received a UF-City of Gainesville grant for Data-centric Modeling and Support of the Lifecycle of the Gainesville Businesses.

Steve Pearton, MSE, Mark Law, ECE, Erin Patrick, ECE, and Fan Ren, ChemE, won a $1.75M contract from the Defense Threat Reduction Agency to study the basic science of radiation effects in wide bandgap semiconductors, with the goal of incorporating this new knowledge into the design radiation-insensitive power switching and RF generation electronics.

David Arnold, ECE, and Yong-Kyu “YK” Yoon, ECE, won a $1.1M award for the new Magnetic Miniaturized and Monolithically Integrated Components (M3IC) program in the DARPA Microsystems Technology Office. The team will develop thick-film magnetic materials that can be fabricated on semiconductor integrated circuits to enable highly miniaturized microwave components for next generation radios, radar, and sensing systems.

Swarup Bhunia, ECE, Mark Tehranipoor, ECE, Daniela Oliveira, ECE, and Kevin Butler, CISE, received a collaborative $4.6M grant with Florida International University for the NSF Cybercorps Scholarship for Service (SFS) program. The UF FICS team will receive $2.5M to train the next generation of cybersecurity experts.
